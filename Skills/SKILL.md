# Cover Letter skill

When Bharadwaj asks for a cover letter, follow this structure. UK English throughout
(optimisation, behaviour, programme, organised, recognised).

**Keep it under 300 words. Three paragraphs.**

1. **Intention** — why this specific role and this specific company. Reference
   something real about the company. No generic "I am writing to apply" openers.
2. **Proof** — pick 3–4 requirements from the job description. For each: what he did
   → why it matters → how he did it. Use only real evidence: the IJRES Shadow Hand
   paper, the MSc, the 49 Azure badges, the FastAPI/RAG work, the Unity navigation.
   Never invent or inflate.
3. **Close** — short, confident, aligned with the company's mission. End with:
   "I hold a UK Graduate Visa with full right to work in the UK and am available immediately."

Lead with the strongest verifiable differentiator (usually the published paper).
Mirror the job description's language. No tables, no fluff.
